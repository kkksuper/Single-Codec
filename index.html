1. Abstract

The multi-codebook speech codec enables the application of large language models (LLM) in TTS but bottlenecks efficiency and robustness due to multi-sequence prediction. To avoid this obstacle, we propose Single-Codec, a single-codebook single-sequence codec, which employs a disentangled VQ-VAE to decouple speech into a time-invariant embedding and a phonetically-rich discrete sequence. Furthermore, the encoder is enhanced with 1) contextual modeling with the BLSTM module to exploit the temporal information; 2) a hybrid sampling module to alleviate distortion from upsampling and downsampling; 3) a resampling module to encourage discrete units to carry more phonetic information. Compared with multi-codebook codecs, e.g. EnCodec and TiCodec, Single-Codec demonstrates higher reconstruction quality with a lower bandwidth of only 304bps, and enhances TTS in naturalness and intelligibility, validating the effectiveness of the proposed single-codebook codec in LLM-TTS.
